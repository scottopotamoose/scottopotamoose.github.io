In college, I played around with web and game development and enjoyed both immensely.
Unfortunately I had little time to practice these hobbies except during school breaks.
Despite these time restraints, I was able to do all the homework for a friend's CompSci development course.
I also created several websites and all of the Unity tutorial games.
I had a portfolio site around this time with completed projects, but I let the hosting lapse when I graduated and instead built a Wix portfolio for my technical writing works.

I then attempted to create my own Contra-esque side-scrolling game.
After investing many hours, I was blocked by not having enough time to learn all of the art tools required.
I don't consider myself an artist by any means, but I did enjoy creating several graphs and icons for school projects, including an entire branding exercise.
I have also been known to redownload GIMP and Photoshop occasionally to make silly things (see picture above).

When I started in cybersecurity, I had just studied for and received the Comptia A+ certification.
Within a year I was a shift lead, and not long after that, I was promoted to associate-level.
I put in the study hours and paid attention to what was going on around me.
After several years of climbing the ladder in cybersecurity, I've realized that I need a more creative and hands-on element in my career.

Even with a full plate, I've loved spending time exploring web development again.
So far, I've completed two video courses on Udemy for HTML/CSS/Javascript, built a few test sites, and am most of the way through the second freecodecamp course.
I am excited to be a developer and to learn more and more as the projects become increasingly complex and demanding.
I'm also interested in UX/UI design concepts and making the web even more accessible for people with impairments and disabilities.
